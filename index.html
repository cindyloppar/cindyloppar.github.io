Game of life

The Game of life, also known as life, is a cellular automaton devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970. The game is zero-player game, meaning that it is evolving is determined by it's initial state, requiring no further input. One interacts with the Game of Life by creating an initial configuration and observing how it evolves, or, for advanced players, by creating patterns with particular properties.
